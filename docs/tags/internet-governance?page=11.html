.бг Cyrillic domain registration officially started
Tue 12 Jul 2016, 23:20

New hosting institution exists in Bulgaria, since ICANN approved the Cyrillic domain .бг as an internationalised country code top-level domain. Имена.бг JSC is the company  currently acting as a registrar. Имена.бг is the first domain in Cyrillic in Bulgaria. As of 08.02.2017 an initial sunrise three-month stage for domain name request and registration has started with the purpose of prioritising the registrations of organisations on the grounds of their name (company, registered trade mark). Registering .бг domain itself will be possible after May 8th, 2017. In order to avoid any misuse of state institution’s names, the Ministry of Transport, Information and Communications has elaborated a list of names to be locked for public institutions.

France plans internet ombudsman to safeguard free speech
Mon 19 Dec 2016, 23:39

France is considering appointing an official internet ombudsman to regulate complaints about online material in order to prevent excessive censorship and preserve free speech. A bill establishing a “content qualification assessment procedure” has been tabled in the French Senate and the initiative was debated last week at a high level meeting attended by senators and judges as well as policy officers from Google and Twitter. The aim is to provide a simple procedure that will support firms operating online who are uncertain of their legal liabilities and to prevent over-zealous removal or censorship of material merely because it is the subject of a complaint. It could be copied by other European jurisdictions.

Swiss parliament votes in favor of Internet blocking in the context of online gamling
Fri 3 Mar 2017, 00:41

According to decisions of the Swiss Parliament taken on March 1, 2017, offline and online gambling will be regulated by limiting it to a fixed number of Swiss-based operators only. Foreign online gambling sites will be considered illegal, and Internet service providers will be obliged to block these sites based on IP addresses or domain names. This is the first law in Switzerland which foresees such measures for any purpose. Critics warn about a “slippery slope” of increasing demands for Internet blocking that could even amount to censorship.
