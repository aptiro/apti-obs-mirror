The Guardia di Finanza blocked 87 pirate websites
Fri 25 Mar 2016, 15:21

On May 26, the Guardia di Finance (Italian finance police) seized 87 web sites unlawfully transmitting and disseminating proprietary content owned by the pay-TVs of Sky and Mediaset. It is one of the biggest operations of such kind. Charges have been pressed against 145 individuals and 7 societies as buyers of the contents protected by IPR (article 171ter – comma 2 Italian IPR Law).

An ongoing debate on Digital IPR protection
Mon 1 Feb 2016, 15:25

There is an ongoing debate on how the Law is dealing with the issue of IPR protection in the digital world. One emerging issue concerns the need to involve the internet provider in the activities against piracy and in the fight against IPR violation. An example is the judgement of January 13, 2016 passed by the Sezione Specializzata Impresa del Tribunale di Milano (Specialised Enterprise Section of the Court of Milan), by which the Court orders to the internet provider to block the access to Rojadirecta web site. Other judgements calling for an involvement of internet providers, have been passed by the relevant Sections of the Court of Milan, as well as by the Court of Turin.

Czech Republic is closer to blocking of gambling websites
Wed 20 Apr 2016, 16:05

A new bill that allows blocking gambling websites without a licence in the Czech Republic has been passed through the Czech Chamber of Deputies. The bill is in now the Senate who can suggest changes but these can be overridden by the Chamber of Deputies. Besides traditional reasoning of regulation’s opponents, the bill is controversial because the black list of websites will be created and maintained by decision of the Ministry of finance and not by the order of court.

Swiss parliament votes in favor of Internet blocking in the context of online gamling
Fri 3 Mar 2017, 00:41

According to decisions of the Swiss Parliament taken on March 1, 2017, offline and online gambling will be regulated by limiting it to a fixed number of Swiss-based operators only. Foreign online gambling sites will be considered illegal, and Internet service providers will be obliged to block these sites based on IP addresses or domain names. This is the first law in Switzerland which foresees such measures for any purpose. Critics warn about a “slippery slope” of increasing demands for Internet blocking that could even amount to censorship.
