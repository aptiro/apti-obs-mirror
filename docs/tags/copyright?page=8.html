Spain: Criminal court orders hacker to pay compensation for facilitating downloading movies
Wed 8 Apr 2015, 12:10

The hacker known as Triplorita was convicted for intellectual property infringement and has to pay 167,398 euros to Egeda company and 83,600 euros to Columbia Tristar Entertainment.

Between March 2009 and February 2010 he facilitated downloading copies of newly released movies in theaters.

New home copying levies
Tue 23 Dec 2014, 14:24

Home copying charges will fall by 30%. This means a levy of €3.50 on desktop PCs, notebooks, smartphones, telephones with MP3 players, tablets, HDD recorders and set-top boxes with a hard drive. A levy of €1.40 will be imposed for portable audio/video players. An amount of €0.70 will be levied for external HDD drives and e-readers. The lowest rate of €0.02 will apply when purchasing a CD-ROM or DVD.

Abolition of the protection of scripts
Tue 23 Dec 2014, 14:27

From the start of the new year 2015, the Copyright Law will only protect works of an original nature which bear the personal stamp of the author. The protection of non-original scripts in the case of, for example, telephone directories, catalogues, schedules, theatre programmes and instruction manuals will be abolished. Information can be taken from these verbatim.

State settles with the Stichting de Thuiskopie (Home Copy Foundation) on remuneration for private copies
Mon 8 Dec 2014, 14:30

The State and the Home Copy Foundation have ended their dispute over the amount of the charge for private copies for the period 2007-2012. They have now agreed on a compensation amount of €33.5 million to be paid by the State to the Home Copy Foundation. The legal proceedings being conducted on this issue between the Home Copy Foundation and the State will now be terminated.

Orphan works to be published on-line more easily
Tue 11 Mar 2014, 15:01

Copyright works - such as books, daily newspapers, image or sound fragments - which are available in institutions accessible to the public, such as libraries, educational institutions, museums - also referred to as 'heritage institutions' - can soon be published on-line more easily. This is evident from a bill by State Secretary Teeven (Security and Justice) and Minister Bussemaker (Education, Culture and Science) which transposes a European directive into Dutch law. The regulations have been submitted to the House of Representatives.
