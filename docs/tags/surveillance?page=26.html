Fri 28 Aug 2015, 15:44

The Dutch cabinet’s plans to give the security service far greater powers to tap phones and internet services are impossible to implement and too far reaching, according to telecoms company Tele2. Home affairs minister Ronald Plasterk put his plans out to consultation earlier this month and Tele2 is the first of the big Dutch telcos to respond. The new rules will give the AIVD and MIVD access to all fixed link and internet traffic in what critics say is a Big Brother charter.
