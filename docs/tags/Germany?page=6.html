Number plate scanners and body cams for officers of the Federal Police Depar
Tue 16 May 2017, 18:02

The “Bundestag” passed a law allowing the Federal Police Department (Bundespolizei) the usage of body cams for their duty. Body cams shall enhance the security of police officers which are more and more subject of violent assaults. According to the current security situation, the Federal Police Department is also entitled to use number plate scanners in order to enhance the capabilities in cases where a danger is caused by a suspect being on the run.

Access to passport photos for intelligence agencies
Thu 9 Mar 2017, 18:04

A draft law granting intelligence agencies access to passport photos was discussed in the “Bundestag”. According to the draft law, the Federal Intelligence Service (Bundesnachrichtendienst), the Federal Office for the Protection of the Constitution, State Offices for the Protection of the Constitution and the Military Counter-Intelligence Service shall be granted access to the passport photos through an automated procedure. In the current version of the respective law, access is only granted to police authorities.

Liability for links: First decisions after the ECJ judgement
Thu 8 Dec 2016, 18:07

A decision of the district court Hamburg caused legal uncertainty by following the arguments of the latest ECJ judgement: According to the district court, a person operating a website that contains a link to illegal content is liable for this content as long as he somehow intents to realize a profit by running the website. This would potentially affect all companies and freelancer running own websites.

Draft law for copyright regarding science published
Thu 12 Jan 2017, 18:18

A draft law for adjusting regulations in the copyright law regarding science was published by the Ministry of Justice. The law contains a list of conditions under which a protected work might be, for instance, copied for scientific purposes. A general clause, as it was proposed before, was not implemented in the draft law.

Secret documents reveal: BND attacked Tor and advises not to use it
Wed 20 Sep 2017, 18:40

The German spy agency BND developed a system to monitor the anonymity network Tor and warned federal agencies that its anonymity is “ineffective”. This is what emerges from a series of secret documents published by the German Netzpolitik blog. The spies handed a prototype of this technology over to the US National Security Agency (NSA), in expectation of a favour in return.

Controversial testing of facial recognition software in Germany
Wed 6 Sep 2017, 18:20

At the end of August 2017, German police has been testing a facial recognition software at Südkreuz train station in Berlin. The system was tested on 300 volunteers. The goal was to evaluate the accuracy of the software in recognising and distinguishing them from the crowd – a feature that the police hopes to ultimately use to track and arrest crime and terrorism suspects.
