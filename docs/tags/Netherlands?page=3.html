Dutch Supreme court: inventive steps matter, not others
Fri 3 Oct 2014, 14:58

The Supreme Court decided on October 3 2014 that it is not relevant whether the objective technical problem to which the invention offers a solution would have been recognized by the skilled artisan. What is relevant is whether the invention does not result from the state of the art in an obvious manner. With this remark, the Supreme Court dismissed Leo Pharmaceutical's cassation appeal and endorsed the decision of the Appeal Court in which EP679154 is deemed to lack inventive step.

Court rules on right to be delisted from search engines
Wed 15 Apr 2015, 12:58

On 12 February 2015, the Amsterdam Court ruled on a case where the plaintiff requested that Google modify its search results based on search queries containing certain words including, but not limited to the plaintiff’s name. This is the second occasion where the Amsterdam Court has been asked to rule on this subject, popularly referred to as the “right to be forgotten”.

Net Neutrality Guidelines in the Netherlands come into force on March 15, 2015
Wed 27 May 2015, 15:00

The Dutch Department of Economic Affairs issued guidelines on net neutrality for the Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM). These Net Neutrality Guidelines serve as a basis for the enforcement by ACM of the net neutrality rules in Article 7.4a of the Dutch Telecommunications Act (Net Neutrality Act).

Dutch telecos KPN and Vodafone fined for net neutrality violations
Tue 27 Jan 2015, 17:00

The Dutch Authority for Consumer and Market (ACM) said it fined KPN because the free wi-fi hotspots it provided at Dutch airports "blocked various services including several internet calling services. As a result, consumers were unable to use these services. Internet providers are prohibited from deciding what their customers can and cannot do online." And the Reason to fine Vodafone is the positive discrimination it exerted on consumers in general on its network by offering applications allowing customers to watch pay-TV channel HBO for free on certain conditions.

Dutch Scientific Council for Government Policy (WRR) published report on Internet
Tue 31 Mar 2015, 18:00

The report is sent to the Dutch Minister of Foreign Affairs, Bert Koenders regarding internet-related foreign policy. The report recommends the promotion of the norm that the core of the Internet - the central protocols and infrastructure that are a global public good - must be free of government interference; the promotion of different forms of security in relation to the Internet distinguished from each other nationally and internationally and are addressed by separate actors; and the expansion of the diplomatic field a part of the agenda for Internet diplomacy.
