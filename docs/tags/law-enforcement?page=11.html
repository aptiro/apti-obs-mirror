Number plate scanners and body cams for officers of the Federal Police Depar
Tue 16 May 2017, 18:02

The “Bundestag” passed a law allowing the Federal Police Department (Bundespolizei) the usage of body cams for their duty. Body cams shall enhance the security of police officers which are more and more subject of violent assaults. According to the current security situation, the Federal Police Department is also entitled to use number plate scanners in order to enhance the capabilities in cases where a danger is caused by a suspect being on the run.

EDPS recommendations for the police and justice sectors
Wed 28 Oct 2015, 17:00

The European Data Protection Supervisor issued an opinion containing recommendations for the police and justice sectors. EDPS recalls that data protection in the police and justice sectors should be fully consistent with the general rules contained in the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and should only contain specifications and adjustments where necessary in view of the specific nature of these sectors. The scope of the Directive should be limited to the areas where specific rules are really necessary, namely the activities of criminal law enforcement by police and judicial authorities, as was the  case  in  the  original  proposal  of  the Commission. Moreover, the performance of law enforcement tasks by non-public entities and organisations should be subject to the GDRP.

Denmark: Targeted ANPR data retention turned into mass surveillance
Wed 6 Sep 2017, 18:20

Since mid 2016, Denmark has a nationwide automatic number plate recognition (ANPR) system with stationary cameras at 24 locations and mobile cameras mounted on 48 police cars. The ANPR system is currently being integrated with POL-INTEL, the new Danish system for intelligence-led policing (predictive policing), which is supplied by Palantir Technologies. Expansion of the ANPR system with more cameras can be expected in the coming years.

Portuguese Government authorizes sound recordings using public surveillance cameras in Lisbon
Tue 3 Oct 2017, 10:16

The Ministry for Internal Administration (MAI) of the Portuguese government has green-lighted a proposal to gather live audio streams coming from the public surveillance cameras now under the management of the Public Security Police (PSP) and the National Republican Guard, the two main LEAs operating in the state.

The operation is restricted for now to a Lisbon district (Barrio Alto) and will not require any judicial order.
