German court asks CJEU what a 'quotation' is: only unaltered reproductions or also something else?
Thu 27 Jul 2017, 15:40
Germany's Federal Court of Justice has referred a new copyright case to the Court of Justice of the European Union: I ZR 228/15 - Reformistischer Aufbruch.

According to the relevant press release (and its Google Translate translation), similarly to the two other references mentioned above, also this new case seeks guidance on – among other things – the interplay between copyright protection and the protection of third-party rights and freedoms (including freedom of the press), as well as the proper interpretation of relevant exceptions in the InfoSoc Directive – in this case, news reporting and quotation within, respectively, Article 5(3)(c) and (d) of the InfoSoc Directive.

Federal Network Agency investigates possible violation of net neutrality by Vodafone
Fri 10 Feb 2017, 17:39

Vodafone announced to throttle the bandwidth for certain websites (e.g. one-click-hoster such as uploaded.net or mega.nz) if an overload of the network is happening. According to Vodafone, browsing the web or using social media platforms would take the priority in this case. Whether this might be a violation to the net neutrality rules is subject to an investigation through the Federal Network Agency.

New regulation enhancing transparency for information about internet connections
Thu 1 Dec 2016, 17:43

A new regulation passed by the “Bundestag” enforces internet access providers to provide customers with information about the expected bandwidth of an internet connection before a contract is signed. This information shall include the minimum and maximum bandwidth, as well as the expected average. Internet Access Providers are also obliged to inform customers about the actual bandwidth available once the connection is available.

Law for enhancing video surveillance passed by the Bundestag
Fri 10 Mar 2017, 17:59

The “Bundestag” passed a law for enhancing video surveillance in public places such as stadiums, night clubs or shopping centres for the purpose of public security and for preventing terrorist attacks. Therefore, the law aims at enhancing the public-private partnership by lowering the safeguards for video surveillance in locations which belong to private institutions. To this extent, DPAs should give the security interests (i.e. the protection of people’s life, health and freedom) more weight compared to the privacy rights of citizens.
