UN appoints special rapporteur on right to privacy
Fri 3 Jul 2015, 20:40

The President of the UN Human Rights Council has selected Mr. Joseph Cannataci to serve as the first UN Special Rapporteur on the Right to Privacy. Cannataci is chair of European Information Policy and Technology Law at the University of Groningen in The Netherlands. He has served as an expert for panels on privacy, data protection, the Internet and cyber crime for the Council of Europe, the European Commission, and UNESCO.

The UN Internet Governance Forum was the most important product of the World Summit on the Information Society (WSIS). UN council passed online freedom resolution
Mon 4 Jul 2016, 11:51

A UN resolution entitled "The promotion, protection and enjoyment of human rights on the Internet" effectively extends human rights held offline to the internet. It was passed by consensus, but only after a determined effort by a number of countries, including China and Russia, to pull out key parts of the text.
